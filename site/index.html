System summary

On time = arrived at the scheduled time, up to 3 min late (0 ≤ delay ≤ 3 min).

Early = before scheduled time (delay < 0).

Late = more than 3 min after schedule (delay > 3 min).

Cumulative on-time at looser thresholds

Same numerator (arrived at-or-after schedule), but counting anyone who arrived within the larger window.

Within 5 min = 0 ≤ delay ≤ 5 min.

Within 7 min = 0 ≤ delay ≤ 7 min.

Delay distribution (1-min buckets)

Each bar = one whole-minute bucket of the schedule-vs-actual delay, counted across all observed stop arrivals.

Outliers beyond −15 min or +45 min are clamped to the boundary buckets.

Schedule compliance

Service delivered = percent of scheduled stop-arrivals that occurred within the rider-experience window: no more than 1 minute early and no more than 7 minutes late (−60 s ≤ delay ≤ 420 s). Stops outside that window — whether the bus never came, ran too early, or ran too late — count against the score. This is intentionally stricter than an operator's internal on-time definition: a bus that runs 10 minutes late delivered its trip but did not deliver the service a rider waiting at the stop needed. Colour scale: green ≥ 95%, orange at 90%, red at 80–75%, darkest red ≤ 65%.

Observed running = trips with at least one recorded stop arrival (regardless of delay). Dropped trips = scheduled trips with no observed arrivals at all.

Trips not completed = trips that ran but never reached their final scheduled stop (bus broke down, was reassigned mid-trip, or GPS dropped out before the end of the route).

How far did "not completed" trips get?

For each trip counted as "not completed," what fraction of its scheduled stops were observed (last_observed_stop_seq / final_stop_seq). A heavy tail near 90–100% means buses are bailing on the very last stop or two; a flatter shape means GPS is dropping mid-route.

Headway distortion

For each observed stop arrival, we compare the bus's lateness/earliness to the scheduled gap to its neighboring scheduled trip at the same stop on the same route.

Late buses use the prior scheduled trip's gap; early buses use the next.

Example (late): a 10-min headway with a bus 6 min late = +60% distortion (the gap from the previous bus stretched from 10 to 16 min).

Example (early): a 5-min-early arrival on a 5-min headway = −100% distortion — the bus collapsed onto the previous scheduled slot.

Higher absolute values = more disruptive to riders waiting at the stop.
